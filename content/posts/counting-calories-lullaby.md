Here's a lullaby for all my fellow foodies. Also... For anyone who managed to survive 2020. I feel like since March all anyone I know has done is sit and eat. I know I gained a bunch of pandemic weight that I'm working to get off. 

I will say that my weakness in breakfast food. In particular, I LOVE pancakes. Before the pandemic hit we did the "Tour du Pancakes" where I ate at several local restaurants. I highly reccomend this experience. I hope to do round two once the pandemic ends. 

## Perkins
![Perkins Pancakes](/images/posts/2021-02-19-Counting-Calories/perkins.jpg)

We started off the tour with an old pancake staple, Perkins. This was on "All You Can Eat Pancake" night. 

You can tell what these are just by looking at 'em. Standard run-of-the-mill buttermilk pancakes. Nothing to write home about. They take three days to digest. 

**Pros**
- All you can eat
- Cheap
- Friendly service

**Cons**
- Only slightly more edible than cardboard. 

## Cracker Barrell
![Cracker Barrell Pancakes](/images/posts/2021-02-19-Counting-Calories/cracker_barrell.jpg)

These were pecan pancakes. While they were a slight step up from Perkins, they also were a bit overcooked. I'm not a fan of crispy pancakes. On the other hand, the Cracker Barrell hashbrowns are pretty legit. 

**Pros**
- More flavorful than Perkins. 
- Hashbrown side was welcome. 
- The peg game was a fun distraction before we all had to become germ obsessed by necessity. 

**Cons**
- Crispy
- Pretty sure the peg game was never sanitary in the first place.

## Blueberry Cafe

![Blueberry Cafe Pancakes](/images/posts/2021-02-19-Counting-Calories/blueberry_cafe.jpg)

This is a local establishment which, if you're ever in the Dayton area, you MUST visit. Here's their [Facebook Page](https://www.facebook.com/TheBlueberryCafe/). Pro tip... Call ahead 30 minutes prior to your arrival to reduce your wait time to get in. 

This pancake is MASSIVE and DELISH! The hashbrowns are good too. The best food never comes from chain restaurants. If you want some amazing breakfast comfort food, this is where to go. 

**Pros**
- Massive pancake size
- Delicious
- Good service despite being perpetually busy

**Cons**
- Sugar-free syrup wasn't available during my visit. If this is a concern for you, pack your own. They may have just been out that day though. 

## Clifton Mill

![Clifton Mill Pancakes](/images/posts/2021-02-19-Counting-Calories/clifton_mill.jpg)

Another local establishment that I highly reccomend. Here's their [website](https://cliftonmill.com/). They also decorate the place up big time for Christmas every year. 

When I ate there they had a thing where if you ate the first two pancakes, you got the third one free. They tried to put my third one in a to-go box. I told them to forget that and ate all three in one sitting. 

This was really good, especially since I like cornmeal. The pancake size is no joke. Make sure you bring your appetitie if you plan on getting that third pancake. lol!

**Pros**
- Cornmeal pancakes are awesome. 
- Fun restaurant atmosphere. 
- Free third pancake if you're a glutton like me. 

**Cons**
- The massive shit you have to take the next day after eating three massive pancakes. 

# Waffle House

![Waffle House](/images/posts/2021-02-19-Counting-Calories/waffle_house.jpg)

I would argue that waffles are just pancakes with abs, therefore I have no issues including Waffle House on this list. 

You've eaten at Waffle House. You know what this is. Somehow it's even more amazing when you're drunk at 2AM. 

**Pros**

- Open at 2AM. 
- Best sugar free syrup I've ever found. 
- OMG BEST HASHBROWNS EVR!

**Cons**
- Getting into arguments with people about whether a waffle qualifies as a pancake.
